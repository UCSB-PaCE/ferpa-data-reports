A broad showcase of one pattern at work: scripts do the repeatable counting and shaping, a language model is brought in only for the part a script cannot do (turning free text into a fair, cited summary), and a person approves what ships. Most examples are from UCSB departments; others show how the same arc fits any campus or organization. Each card credits the team whose project inspired it.

Most cards link to a downloadable synthetic sample you can open in Google Colab or a spreadsheet to try the pipeline before you point it at your own data. Every sample is made up: no real records, names, or figures.

Every UCSB example here comes from the AI@Work Certificate Program, a free, cohort-based program that builds UCSB employees' capacity to apply AI in their daily work, responsibly, ethically, and sustainably. Each card began as a capstone project there, with thanks to the program, its organizers, and the inaugural cohort.

Transaction reconciliation

Department financial analysts

You have

Hundreds of multi-line card transactions (charges, reversals, allocations) across several reports

↓
You want

An exception report flagging what needs review, and optionally the whole match automated

Scripts do all the matching and the math, grouping the charge, reversal, and allocation lines; the model only writes the plain-language note for each flagged mismatch. Accessibility and media teams

You have

Dense presentation decks of graphs and equations that need descriptive text

↓
You want

More AI wins with campus tools

Not every win is a data-to-report pipeline. These are other things UCSB staff built with campus AI tools: automations, assistants, accessibility helpers, and process work. Similar projects are grouped and credited together.

A note on data: match the tool to your data's protection level. On UCSB accounts, Google and Gemini are approved up to P4, and LLM Sandbox is a campus-run gateway option. Check the official GenAI tools at UCSB list and AI use guidelines before using real records.
UCSB · Automation

Automate a repetitive workflow

Non-coders with a manual, repetitive process

Used AI to write the code (often Google Apps Script) that automates a repetitive process end to end, with a human approval gate. The same approach handled quarterly course scheduling, room bookings, graduate onboarding emails, mapping inventory into a rigid system template, and consolidating leads from many tools into one live sheet. Real data stays out of the model; the AI writes the script, you run it.
