The AI Prism

Refracting administrative complexity into operational brilliance

An accessible recreation of the cohort showcase: the innovations, results, and wins of 25 transformative campus AI projects from UCSB's inaugural AI@Work cohort.

Created by Claudio Alunan with Gemini Gems and NotebookLM, and recreated here as an accessible page. The original is a visual slide deck; this version is real, screen-reader-friendly text.

How this deck was made (the prompt) 
Claudio's method: he wrote an initial prompt in his own words, had Gemini turn it into a structured "Gem" prompt, then re-tweaked the wording the model got wrong, an alignment between how the model wants to understand the task and how the human wants the deliverable. He then asked it to group the output by theme (it found five), and fed that into NotebookLM with one instruction: make it vibrant and fun.

THE GEM PROMPT
Purpose and Goals:
- Act as an 'AI Expert' specializing in academic project synthesization to transform source documents into clear, professional slide deck summaries.
- Synthesize complex AI project documents into concise, high-impact bullet points for academic personnel.
- Structure every summary across three specific categories: Current State and Desired Change, Approach and Iteration Process, and Results and Looking Ahead. Emphasize Wins if there are any.

Behaviors and Rules:
1) Clarification Phase:
   a) Before generating any summaries, you must ask exactly 3 targeted questions to clarify the project's details or the user's specific focus areas within the source document.
   b) Do not provide the summary output until the user has provided answers or feedback to these 3 questions.
2) Summary Generation:
   a) Create a slide deck summary for each presenter mentioned in the source document.
   b) Each summary must include these categories: Background, Goal, Approach, Testing, Wins, and Future Project.
   c) Ensure each category uses clear, concise bullet points suitable for a professional presentation.
   d) Focus strictly on the provided source document for all content. Do not include external information, outside knowledge, or 'hallucinations'.
3) Audience Alignment:
   a) Tailor the language for 'academic personnel cohorts' to showcase the benefits of AI.
   b) Explicitly highlight how the AI projects facilitate 'process improvements'.

Overall Tone: Professional, analytical, and authoritative. Academic and highly structured. Precise and strictly grounded in the provided source material.

The deployment matrix

The AI engine diagnostic: which tool when?

Distilling the cohort's comparative testing into an actionable deployment matrix.

NotebookLM

Proven by Emi Sautot, Martina Ubaldo, LeeAnn Trusela

Limitation: lacks precise prioritizing logic for complex routing.

Custom Gemini Gems

Proven by Debi Vance, Celine Teng, Julia Choi

Best for: collaborative brainstorming, custom script generation, and rapid integration into existing Google workflows.

Limitation: needs strict boundary prompting to avoid context drift.

The operating truth

The human-AI collaboration framework

Across 25 distinct projects, one truth held: AI was never granted agency to make final decisions. It was used strictly to augment and elevate human judgment.

AI processing engine→Human gatekeeper

Strict validation

AI requires mandatory secondary human validation for sensitive constraints (for example, unpublished sabbatical research or institutional procurement data).

Preserving agency

AI diagnoses and scores localized datasets (like non-traditional applicant rubrics), but the absolute authority to accept or reject remains fundamentally human.

Directed curiosity

The most successful prompts forced AI to flag its own uncertainties, directing human reviewers exactly where to apply their localized expertise.
